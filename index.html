Most Popular Domestic Cat Breeds
  1. Siamese

    Fun fact: this is my favorite cat breed. I saw them in Lady and the Tramp and fell in love with the breed (which was definitely not the intended purpose of the writers). They originated in Thailand, which is where they got their name: the Siam-ese. They have distinct markings called "points" where they are colored.

  2. Persian

    Their distinctive smushed face makes them either an instant hit or miss, but they also possess luxuriously long fur coats that can come in almost any color.

  3. Maine Coon

    As is quite obvious from the images above, I adore Maine Coons. They are the largest domestic cat breed, holding the record of the largest cat ever recorded at 4ft long.

  4. Ragdoll

    These cats got their name from their propensity to go limp when picked up, much like a rag doll. They are about the size of a medium sized dog with the temperament to match.

  5. Bengal

    These felines got their name from their distinctive markings that make them look like their much larger and much more deadly wild cousins: leopards and tigers.

  6. Abyssinian

    These cats have a distinctive red/orange coat that is actually threaded through with lighter and darker bands.

  7. Birman

    They look quite similar to the Siamese and Ragdoll but have no undercoat.

  8. Oriental Shorthair

    These are some weird looking boys. They usually have piercing green eyes but may also come in other colors.

  9. Sphynx

    The Sphyx cat is physically incapable of shedding (because they have no fur to shed), which gives them a very unique look.

  10. Devon Rex

    Initially, I thought that these cats looked incredibly strange because of their wavy coat.
